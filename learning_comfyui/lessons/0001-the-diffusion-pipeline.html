Lesson 1 · Concepts

The Diffusion Pipeline

Before you wire a single node, here is the machine you're operating — and why every ComfyUI graph is the same six boxes underneath.

Your mission is to turn stories into manga panels you fully control. Control starts with a mental model. By the end of this page you'll be able to look at any ComfyUI text-to-image workflow — yours or one you downloaded from Reddit — and name what each part is doing. That's the foundation every later lesson stands on.

1. The one big idea: generation is denoising

A diffusion model does not "draw." During training it watched millions of images get destroyed by static, step by step, until each was pure noise — and it learned to run that backwards. So to make a picture, it starts from a screen of random noise and removes a little bit at a time, guided by your prompt, until an image is left behind. (plain-English explainer)

Think of a sculptor with a block of marble: the statue is "already in there," and each chip reveals more of it. Your prompt is the sculptor's intent; the noise is the marble.

2. Where it happens: latent space

Doing this on raw pixels would be brutally slow. So the model works in latent space — a compressed code of the image about 48× smaller than the pixels. A 512×512 picture becomes a tiny 64×64×4 grid of numbers called a latent. The whole noisy-to-clean dance happens on that small grid, then gets expanded back to pixels at the very end.

Why this matters for your M1 Latent space is the reason a 16 GB Mac can generate images at all. It's also why SD1.5 and SDXL run comfortably on your machine while pixel-space methods never could.

3. The four players

Four components do all the work. Memorise these four and the nodes will make instant sense.

Here's the punchline. The default ComfyUI text-to-image workflow is just these six boxes, left to right. Every fancy graph you'll ever see is this spine with extra nodes clipped on.

NodeWhat it really does
Load CheckpointOpens your model file and hands out its three parts: CLIP, U-Net (labelled MODEL), and VAE.
CLIP Text EncodeEncodes one prompt. You use two: positive (what you want) and negative (what to avoid).
Empty Latent ImageCreates the blank noisy latent and sets the image dimensions (e.g. 512×512 for SD1.5).
KSamplerThe engine room. Takes model + both prompts + the latent, then runs the denoise loop. Home of seed, steps, CFG, sampler.
VAE DecodeExpands the finished latent back into real pixels.
Save ImageWrites the PNG to disk — with the whole workflow embedded inside it.
The KSampler is where you'll live Almost all your "make it look right" tuning happens on four KSampler dials: seed (reproducibility), steps (refinement vs. time), CFG (how literal), and sampler (the algorithm). Lesson 3 is all dials.
